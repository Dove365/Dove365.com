CRM Starter overview

Start here with Dove365 CRM Starter

A practical CRM built on Microsoft Power Platform and Dataverse for small and medium-sized teams that need one place to manage relationships, pipeline, service work, activities, and files.

Where to begin

  1. 1Start with Accounts and Contacts. They are the relationship foundation.
  2. 2Use Leads for early enquiries before they are qualified.
  3. 3Use Opportunities for qualified sales work and add Service Offerings.
  4. 4Use Cases for support requests, service issues, and customer follow-up.
Purpose of the app

A clean CRM for teams outgrowing spreadsheets

Dove365 CRM Starter gives your team a structured way to capture customer information, progress leads, manage opportunities, categorise services, resolve cases, and keep activity history in one place. It is designed to be useful quickly, without enterprise CRM complexity.

Relationship

Accounts

Organisations and companies you work with, including related contacts, opportunities, cases, activities, and files.

Relationship

Contacts

People linked to accounts, leads, opportunities, cases, email tracking, timelines, and SharePoint documents where configured.

Pipeline

Early-stage prospects managed through New, Research, Engage, Qualify, and Convert or Close.

Pipeline

Opportunities

Qualified sales work managed through Qualify, Discovery, Proposal, Negotiate, and Won or Lost.

Service

Cases

Support requests and service matters with priority, type, target dates, escalation, and resolution tracking.

Recommended first steps

Use the app in this order

For new users

Learn the app by following the natural customer journey.

  1. Open the Dashboard and navigation.
  2. Create or review an Account.
  3. Create or review a Contact.
  4. Create a Lead and add activities.
  5. Convert or close the Lead.
  6. Create an Opportunity and add Service Offerings.
  7. Create a Case and record resolution.

For admins

Set up the operational foundations before asking users to go live.

  1. Assign Dove365 CRM User and Admin roles.
  2. Configure service account and mailbox settings.
  3. Confirm SharePoint document management for Contacts.
  4. Create standard Service Offerings.
  5. Confirm business process flows are active.
  6. Validate dashboards, activities, and email tracking.
What to remember

Keep it simple, consistent, and visible

Use the right record

Leads are for early prospects, Opportunities are for qualified sales work, and Cases are for service/support matters.

Keep relationships linked

Connect records to the right Account, Contact, Opportunity, and Service Offering so reporting is reliable.

Trust the timeline

Activities, emails, calls, notes, and tasks should tell the story of what happened and what needs doing next.
