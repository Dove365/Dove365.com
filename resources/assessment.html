A practical self-assessment for your organisation

Artificial intelligence can support productivity, service delivery and better decision-making, but successful adoption involves more than choosing a tool. Organisations also need clear goals, capable people, reliable information and appropriate safeguards.

This checklist helps UK small and medium-sized businesses, charities and schools assess their current position. It is a practical conversation starter, not a technical audit or a formal compliance assessment.

How to use this checklist: Complete it with a small group representing leadership, operations, IT or Microsoft 365 administration, and the people who perform the work being considered for AI.

Existing Microsoft 365 Usage

Microsoft 365 is used consistently across the organisation.
Teams, SharePoint and OneDrive have clear and understood purposes.

User accounts and access permissions are reviewed regularly.
We understand which Microsoft 365 services and Copilot capabilities are available to us.

Data Quality

Important information is accurate enough to support day-to-day decisions.
We know where authoritative versions of key documents and records are held.
Duplicate, outdated and incomplete information is managed.
Business records have clear owners who are responsible for their quality.
We understand which information is personal, sensitive or confidential.

Section 6

Security and Governance

Multi-factor authentication is used where appropriate.
Access to files, sites and systems follows people's job requirements.
We have policies covering acceptable technology and data use.
Employees know what information they may and may not enter into AI tools.
Employees know they must not enter organisational data into personal or unapproved AI accounts.
We have reviewed how approved AI tools use prompts, uploaded files and chat history, including any settings relating to model training or product improvement.
We can assess privacy, security, legal and sector-specific requirements before an AI trial.
We have a process for reporting incorrect, harmful or inappropriate AI results.
Important: This checklist does not replace advice from your data protection, safeguarding, legal, HR, IT or information security specialists.
Watch for shadow AI: Employees may use personal ChatGPT accounts or other consumer AI services when an approved business tool or clear guidance is not available. Identify which tools are being used, provide a safe route for legitimate needs and explain why organisational information must not be entered into unapproved services.
